Right to Equality (Article 18): The law is the same for everyone. Whether you are rich, poor, male, female, or from any ethnic group, the government must treat you equally.
Right to Freedom (Article 17): You are free to speak your mind, meet peacefully with others, move anywhere in Nepal, and start any legal business you like.
Rights Regarding Education (Article 31): Schooling is free and compulsory up to Class 8, and free up to Class 12 in government schools. You also have the right to learn in your mother tongue.
Right to Health (Article 35): You have the right to basic healthcare from the state for free. In case of an emergency, no hospital can turn you away.
Right to Justice (Article 20): If you are arrested, you must be told why within 24 hours and allowed to talk to a lawyer. You are "innocent until proven guilty."
Right to Life and Dignity (Article 16): You have the right to live a respectful life. The government cannot give anyone the death penalty, and no one is allowed to treat you cruelly.
Right Against Untouchability (Article 24): No one can treat you as "untouchable" or discriminate against you because of your caste. This applies to both public and private places.
Right to Information (Article 27): You have the right to ask the government for information on any public matter (like how a bridge is being built or where tax money is going).
Right to Communication (Article 19): The government cannot stop the news, the internet, or radio just because they do not like what is being said. Your phone and letters are private.
Right to Freedom of Religion (Article 26): You can follow and practice any religion you believe in. However, you cannot force someone else to change their religion.
Right to Food (Article 36): No citizen should go hungry. You have the right to enough food to stay healthy, and the state must protect you from food shortages.
Right to Housing (Article 37): You have the right to a safe place to live. The government cannot kick you out of your own home without a legal reason and proper compensation.
Right to Employment (Article 33 and 34) - You have the right to employment as well as labor rights including fair wages, healthy work environment, and social security.
Rights of Women (Article 38): Women have equal rights to family property. They have the right to safe healthcare during pregnancy and protection from any physical or mental abuse.
Rights of Children (Article 39): Every child has the right to a name, a birth certificate, and to be raised with love and care, not work or violence.
Rights of Dalits (Article 40): Dalits get extra support in education, health, and jobs to ensure they have an equal standing in society.
Rights of Seniors, Disabled & Minorities (Article 41-43): The government must provide "social security" and special facilities to help these groups live a comfortable and active life.
Right Against Exploitation (Article 29): No one can force you to work against your will, sell you (trafficking), or make children do dangerous work.
Right Against Preventive Detention (Article 23): The police cannot keep you in jail just because they "suspect" you might do something, unless there is a very strong legal reason. You must be treated humanely.
Right to Clean Environment (Article 30): You have the right to live in a clean and healthy environment.
